# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Members of a church community in Rathmalana, Sri Lanka. The primary audience is the girls
and women playing in the doubles tournament, plus their families and friends spectating —
almost all on phones, in a busy sports hall, checking who plays next and how the bracket is
progressing. A secondary user is the single organizer (Ebi / Siva / Lita) who logs in to
update match results live. The event surface speaks to participants and supporters; the
admin surface serves the organizer running the day.

## Product Purpose

One friendly home for the Shuttle Smash Championship 2026: it invites and informs (event
details, entry, contacts), makes the tournament's custom badminton rules unmistakable, and
shows a live single-elimination bracket that everyone can follow in real time. Success is a
spectator finding the next match and the current bracket in seconds on their phone, and the
organizer updating a result in a couple of taps.

## Positioning

The single place for everything about this church tournament — the invitation, the rules,
and the live bracket — in one warm, on-brand experience, not a generic bracket app.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary and secondary CTA: primary is "View the live bracket"; secondary is "Read the rules".
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: "Fellowship, Fun and Good Time."
- Belief ladder: (1) this is our church's event, made for us → (2) I know exactly when, where,
  and how it runs → (3) the rules are clear and fair → (4) I can follow every match live → so
  I show up, play, and cheer.
- Proof on hand: none required — this is a trusted community event, not a conversion funnel.

## Brand Personality

Spirited, welcoming, celebratory. The voice is warm and encouraging with a sporty edge —
it cheers players on rather than selling to them. It should feel like the tournament poster
come to life: bold navy-and-gold, energetic, and unmistakably a community celebration.

## Anti-references

Not a sterile SaaS dashboard or gray "tournament-software" bracket tool. Not corporate
fintech polish. Avoid AI-slop scaffolding: per-section uppercase eyebrows, 01/02/03 section
numbers, identical icon-heading-text card grids, gradient text, and decorative glassmorphism.
The navy-and-gold palette here is the client's committed logo identity (identity-preservation
wins), not a fintech reflex.

## Design Principles

Fellowship first — the design should feel communal and joyful, celebrating players over
mechanics. Clarity on court — event facts and rules must be scannable and unambiguous under
poor lighting and distraction. Live and legible on a phone — the bracket is the beating heart
and must be effortless to read and refresh one-handed. Never generic — lean fully into the
poster's navy/gold identity so it reads as *this* church's event. Signal without relying on
color alone — winners are marked by weight and a check, not hue only.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA: body text ≥ 4.5:1 contrast, large text ≥ 3:1 (verify gold-on-white and
white-on-navy pairings). Mobile-first, comfortable tap targets for use in a sports hall.
Winner/advancement is conveyed by more than color (bold text + check icon) for color-vision
deficiency. Honor `prefers-reduced-motion` for any animation.
